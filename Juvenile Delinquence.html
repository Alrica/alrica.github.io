What is HTJD?

Helping The Juvenile Delinquents is an organization where a group of young individaul come together to help the juvenile delinquents. Most Teenagers bcome delinquents because they do not have a family that they can depend on for love, affection and attention. Our organization gives them a family that they can talk to. There are different activites that we put in place to help them regain their self confidence and self-esteem. Here's some information about juvenile delinquence


What Is Juvenile Delinquency?


Juvenile Delinquency is a young person, particularly a teenager under the age of eighteen, who breaks a state or federal law by commiting a crime.


Causes Of Juvenile Delinquence


Broken Family


A teen adopts moral and ethnical values from hos or her parents and other family members. It goes without saying that family plays a vital role in shaping a teen's behavior and growing his or her personality. However, teens become violent or show signs of juvenile delinquency only when they're facing disturbance at home. Broken or disturbed families with bad relations can cause teens to go astray and become violent. SIngle parents are often busy working, therefore they're not able to spend quality time with their children.This causes teens to seek attention from others, especially their peers.


Lack of communication

Often lack of discourse in the family can lead children to find solace other than homes. When they are not having any communication with their parents or family members at home, they may lose unity, trust, and understanding, which can eventually lower their self-esteem or self confidence, once they feel they're losing their individuality, they tend to do things they shouldn't do to boost their self confidence. They blindly follow their peers and adopt their unhealty lifestyles. They shoplift and consume drugs to look cool in the eyes of their peers.


Lack Of Finances

Young or adult, may lead to a wrong path to improve their finacial conditions. Teens become juvenile delinquents due to lack of finances. When they experience poor economic conditions, thye start engadging in the wrong activites. They may start selling drugs or steal things to improve their economic conditions.


Lack Of Social And Moral Training

Teens who have not been given any social or moral training often lead to juvenile delinquency. It is the parent's duty to teach moral and ethnicl values to their children. They should teach them the difference between right and wrong behavior. Lack of social and moral values can lead children to poor interacton with others and make them less confident. They may become selfish and arrogant. They would not know how to respect the laws of state. Parents often neglect their children and play more focus on working hard to earn money for them. However, while doing so, they forget the importance of spending quality time with their children. In their absence, children tend to spend time with someone who pays attention to them. They may also fall into wrong hands or get involved in a bad company while seeking attention from someone other than their parents.


Usually when a teen commits a small offense, people especially parents, just show momentary disapproval and then forget about it. They just think of it as a one-time mistake made by a child who doesn't know what he or she is doing and that he must have learned his or her lesson and will not attempt the same thing again, however, quite often thsi doesn't turn out to be the case. Failing to show due concern over a teen's delinquent behavior and taking neccessary measures to discourage it can have a serious consequence not just for the teen, but also their parents.
